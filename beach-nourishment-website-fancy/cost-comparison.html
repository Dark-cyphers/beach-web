Cost Comparison

Beach nourishment generally costs between $1 million and $10 million per mile, depending on the location and amount of sand needed.

Compared to seawalls: Seawalls can cost $2 million to $15 million per mile, but they have a much longer lifespan. However, seawalls often cause beach erosion downstream, which can lead to additional costs later.

Overall, beach nourishment is often cheaper in the short term but needs repeated investment, while seawalls are expensive up front but more permanent.
